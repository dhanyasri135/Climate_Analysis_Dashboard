Gap Analysis for Climate Resilience in Megacities

As cities face increasing climate risks, researchers are racing to model resilience in megacities. This dashboard reframes OpenStreetMap through a research lens: what is missing, why it matters, and which mapped features would most improve climate adaptation modeling across 43 global megacities.

Why this matters

Researchers need more than roads and points of interest.

Standard OpenStreetMap coverage is excellent for navigation, but climate adaptation modeling needs infrastructure detail that is usually missing or inconsistently tagged.

  • Flood models need subsurface drainage and barrier data.
  • Heat-risk response needs cooling centers, shade, and green infrastructure.
  • Evacuation planning needs shelter capacity and route capacity attributes.
  • Stormwater resilience needs permeability and retention infrastructure.

Key findings

Only 12 of 43 megacities have good OSM coverage (≥70%)

6 cities have critical gaps (<40% coverage)

Subsurface drainage networks are severely underrepresented in OSM globally

Cooling infrastructure mapping is critically needed for heat adaptation

Emergency shelter capacity data is almost entirely missing from OSM

Permeable surface data could transform stormwater modeling accuracy

Data sources

This analysis combines multiple open and authoritative sources:

  • OpenStreetMap (2025-2026 snapshot) — feature extracts via Overpass API
  • OSM Taginfo — tag usage and completeness statistics
  • Government open data portals (e.g., NYC Open Data, Tokyo Open Data)
  • UN-Habitat Urban Resilience Database
  • C40 Cities Climate Leadership Group datasets
  • World Bank Climate Change Knowledge Portal
  • IPCC regional assessment reports
  • Research literature on VGI and OSM data quality (Haklay; Barrington‑Leigh & Millard‑Ball; Senaratne et al.)

Where available, government datasets were used to validate OSM coverage and to derive feature truth sets.
