📖 Definitions & Glossary

Comprehensive guide to all terms, variables, and statistical concepts used in this analysis

📊 Statistical Terms

R² (R-squared)

Coefficient of determination. Measures how well the regression model fits the data (0-1 scale). R² = 0.95 means 95% of variance in resilience is explained by the model.

Chi-square (χ²)

Statistical test that determines if there's a significant association between two categorical variables. Higher values indicate stronger relationships.

p-value

Probability that results occurred by chance. p < 0.05 indicates statistical significance (less than 5% chance of being random).

Cramer's V

Measure of association strength for categorical variables (0-1 scale). 0.1-0.3 = weak, 0.3-0.5 = moderate, >0.5 = strong association.

Pearson Correlation (r)

Measures linear relationship between two continuous variables (-1 to +1). Positive = both increase together, negative = inverse relationship.

Regression Coefficient

Indicates how much the dependent variable changes when the independent variable increases by one unit. Shows direction and strength of relationship.

🌍 Climate & Risk Variables

Resilience Score

Composite measure (0-100) evaluating a city's capacity to prepare for, respond to, and recover from climate hazards. Based on infrastructure, governance, resources, and adaptation measures.

Flood Risk

Assessment of vulnerability to flooding events (Low/Medium/High). Considers coastal location, elevation, drainage infrastructure, and historical flood patterns.

Heat Risk

Exposure to extreme temperature events (Low/Medium/High). Factors include urban heat island effect, average temperatures, and heatwave frequency.

Drought Risk

Susceptibility to water scarcity (Low/Medium/High). Based on precipitation patterns, water supply infrastructure, and agricultural dependency.

Climate Zone

Geographic classification based on temperature and precipitation patterns. Categories: Tropical, Arid, Temperate, Continental, Polar.

Coastal Location

Binary indicator (Yes/No) of whether the city is located on a coastline, affecting vulnerability to sea-level rise and storm surges.

💰 Socioeconomic Variables

GDP per Capita

Economic output per person (in thousands USD). Indicates financial resources available for climate adaptation infrastructure and emergency response.

Infrastructure Vulnerability

Scale assessing weakness of critical systems (1-10). Evaluates transportation, utilities, healthcare, and communication infrastructure resilience to climate impacts.

Green Space %

Percentage of urban area covered by vegetation. Green spaces reduce heat islands, manage stormwater, and improve air quality.

Stakeholder Engagement

Level of public participation in adaptation planning (Low/Medium/High). Measures community involvement in decision-making processes.

Adaptation Plan Exists

Binary indicator (Yes/No) of whether city has formal climate adaptation strategy. Plans typically include risk assessments and action items.

Early Warning Systems

Binary indicator (Yes/No) of technology/protocols for alerting populations about imminent climate hazards like floods or heatwaves.

💡 Interpretation Guidelines

Correlation Strength
  • |r| < 0.3: Weak correlation
  • |r| 0.3-0.7: Moderate correlation
  • |r| > 0.7: Strong correlation
Statistical Significance
  • p < 0.001: Highly significant (***)
  • p < 0.01: Very significant (**)
  • p < 0.05: Significant (*)
  • p ≥ 0.05: Not significant
Model Quality (R²)
  • R² > 0.9: Excellent fit
  • R² 0.7-0.9: Good fit
  • R² 0.5-0.7: Moderate fit
  • R² < 0.5: Poor fit
Effect Size (Cramer's V)
  • V < 0.1: Negligible effect
  • V 0.1-0.3: Small effect
  • V 0.3-0.5: Medium effect
  • V > 0.5: Large effect
