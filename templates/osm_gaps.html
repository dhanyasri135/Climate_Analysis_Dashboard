💡 Impact Statement

By systematically mapping these critical infrastructure features in OpenStreetMap, we can transform OSM from a navigational tool into a powerful engine for climate adaptation research. This would enable researchers worldwide to model urban resilience, predict cascade failures, optimize evacuation routes, and identify vulnerable populations with unprecedented accuracy—ultimately saving lives and building more sustainable cities.

📊 Data Sources

OSM Coverage Assessment: Based on systematic analysis of OpenStreetMap data via Overpass API queries, OSM Taginfo statistics, and comparative analysis with government infrastructure datasets (2025-2026).

City Climate Data: Integrated from UN-Habitat Urban Resilience Reports, C40 Cities Climate Database, World Bank Climate Risk Assessments, and IPCC Regional Climate Impact Studies.

Gap Identification: Derived from research literature on climate resilience modeling requirements, interviews with climate researchers, and analysis of infrastructure data needs in published adaptation studies.
